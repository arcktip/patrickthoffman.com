About the Author

Patrick T. Hoffman is a product executive, entrepreneur, and writer. He trained as an architect at Drexel and an urban planner at Columbia before spending two decades building products at the intersection of technology and physical space.

His career has spanned Social Bicycles — which became JUMP and was later acquired by Uber — Superpedestrian, Udacity, Google, and Meta. He has built products used by millions, across mobility platforms, enterprise learning, commerce, and advertising infrastructure.

He coaches and mentors product managers through The Product Mentor and has served as a Teaching Fellow at Singularity University. The questions this book tries to answer are the ones he has spent years working through with early-career and mid-career PMs who wanted an honest account of what the job actually is.

He writes at patrickthoffman.com.
