The Premise

What if every belief you hold is a program you didn't write?

In the near-future world of Executable Minds, the dominant scientific paradigm is algorithmic memetics. Every culturally transmitted system of ideas — religions, ideologies, professional worldviews, conspiracy frameworks — is a memeplex: a running program that processes information, applies evaluative weights, and produces behavioral outputs. It does not sit in memory. It runs.

Two people watching the same event will perceive different realities — not because they disagree, but because their cognitive architectures are generating different outputs from identical inputs. Neither person experiences themselves as running a program. Both experience themselves as seeing reality clearly. This divergence is the engine of nearly all conflict in this world.
